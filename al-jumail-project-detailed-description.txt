TIDES OF TIME - AL JUMAIL REVITALIZATION
Detailed Project Description and Program Guide

==================================================================
1) WHAT THE PROJECT IS ABOUT
==================================================================

Project title:
Tides of Time: Reimagining Al Jumail as a Living Memory

Core idea:
This project proposes the revitalization of Al Jumail Village in northern Qatar as an interactive living heritage site. Instead of treating the village as static ruins, the proposal turns the site into a guided cultural landscape where visitors actively experience historical life through participation.

Historical setting:
- Al Jumail developed between the 18th and 19th centuries as a small coastal settlement.
- Its residents depended mainly on fishing and pearling.
- The village was inhabited by local tribal groups and organized around extended-family life, courtyard housing, and a central mosque.
- In the 20th century, the decline of natural pearling and later oil-driven urban migration caused gradual abandonment.

Main challenge the project addresses:
- Physical deterioration of coral-stone architecture due to humidity, salt, wind, and time.
- Cultural and intangible heritage loss, including oral memory and daily practices.
- Limited public understanding of pre-oil coastal life in Qatar.

Project goal:
To preserve the authenticity of Al Jumail while activating it through low-impact, reversible, educational, and culturally grounded programs.

Approach:
- Preserve: Stabilize and protect fragile structures.
- Adapt: Reuse selected spaces with minimal intervention.
- Participate: Let visitors engage through hands-on activities and storytelling.

Design philosophy:
- Not reconstruction of a "new old village."
- Not heavy architectural insertion.
- Controlled activation of existing fabric.
- Heritage interpreted through real practices, social interaction, and movement through place.

==================================================================
2) EXPERIENCE MODEL (HOW VISITORS LEARN)
==================================================================

The site is organized as a story route. Visitors move through linked houses and zones, each representing a part of daily life in historic Al Jumail. Learning happens through doing, seeing, hearing, and discussing, rather than reading only.

Interpretive method:
- House-by-house thematic experience.
- Programmed activities grounded in historical life.
- Guided circulation to protect sensitive areas.
- Integration of memory, labor, hospitality, food, and ecology.

Expected outcomes:
- Better public understanding of coastal heritage.
- Stronger connection between community memory and place.
- A scalable model for heritage activation that balances tourism, conservation, and education.

==================================================================
3) DETAILED HOUSE AND ZONE DESCRIPTIONS
==================================================================

The program includes six primary heritage houses and supporting zones. Below is a detailed description of each mapped destination, including focus/theme and activities.

--------------------------------------------------
A) PEARLING HOUSE
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Maritime labor and pearling practices.

Description:
The Pearling House introduces visitors to the economic and social importance of pearling in coastal Qatar. It presents the full cycle of pearl work, from diving conditions to sorting and trade value, while highlighting the physical intensity and skill involved in the profession.

Activities:
- Oyster opening activity using real tools.
- Handling diving weights and understanding diver equipment.
- Exploring pearl sorting and grading through hands-on displays.
- Experiencing ropes, tools, and materials used in daily pearling work.

Learning intent:
To make visitors understand pearling as lived labor, not just a romantic maritime image.

--------------------------------------------------
B) FAMILY LIFE HOUSE
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Domestic space and daily routines.

Description:
This house presents how families lived, worked, and organized domestic space. It emphasizes social structure, household crafts, and practical routines that shaped everyday life in the settlement.

Activities:
- Participating in textile weaving (sadu).
- Exploring traditional interior layout and family spaces.
- Experiencing everyday household tools and tasks.
- Learning about food preparation and storage practices.

Learning intent:
To show the social and material culture of family life beyond public economic activities.

--------------------------------------------------
C) TRADE HOUSE
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Local exchange systems.

Description:
The Trade House interprets Al Jumail as a regional exchange point rather than a major international port. It explains how village-level barter, goods flow, and basic market logic supported local survival and social exchange.

Activities:
- Interacting with goods such as fish, dates, and pearls.
- Participating in a simple barter-based activity.
- Exploring historic currencies and trade objects.
- Understanding how local exchange supported village life.

Learning intent:
To connect economic life to practical, community-scale transactions.

--------------------------------------------------
D) MAJLIS HOUSE
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Social gathering and hospitality.

Description:
The Majlis House centers on social protocol, conversation, and collective identity. It interprets hospitality as both a cultural value and an everyday social system that structured relationships.

Activities:
- Sitting in a traditional majlis setting.
- Participating in Arabic coffee preparation and serving.
- Listening to guided storytelling and oral history.
- Experiencing hospitality and social interaction practices.

Learning intent:
To reveal how shared space, dialogue, and ritual shaped social life.

--------------------------------------------------
E) AGRICULTURE EXPERIENCE HOUSE
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Resource-limited cultivation and environmental adaptation.

Description:
This house explains how agriculture functioned under difficult environmental conditions. It frames cultivation as adaptive, modest, and tied to survival rather than abundance.

Activities:
- Learning how date palms are used in daily life.
- Exploring small-scale planting adapted to harsh conditions.
- Understanding water use and survival strategies.
- Experiencing basic agricultural tools and techniques.

Learning intent:
To connect heritage knowledge with ecological limits and resilience.

--------------------------------------------------
F) ART & MEMORY HOUSE
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Reflection, memory, and community expression.

Description:
The Art and Memory House provides a space for visitors to reflect on what they learned and contribute their own responses. It links archive, interpretation, and participation through personal and collective memory.

Activities:
- Writing and placing personal messages on a memory wall.
- Viewing archival material and historical references.
- Participating in simple art or drawing activities.
- Contributing to a shared record of visitor experiences.

Learning intent:
To transform the site visit into an ongoing memory practice.

--------------------------------------------------
G) GARDEN & GREENHOUSE
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Contemporary sustainability interpretation.

Description:
This zone introduces hydroponics and water-efficient systems as present-day responses to arid and saline conditions. It is presented as a contemporary educational layer, not as a historical reconstruction of village agriculture.

Activities:
- Demonstration planting.
- Hydroponic cultivation adapted to arid/saline environments.
- Water-efficient closed-loop irrigation demonstrations.

Learning intent:
To bridge heritage interpretation with current sustainability education.

--------------------------------------------------
H) FOOD SPACE
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Local cuisine and social pause.

Description:
The Food Space supports the visitor journey with simple, locally inspired meals tied to the cultural story of the site. It is both a practical service and an interpretive extension of daily life themes.

Activities:
- Serving simple, locally inspired meals.
- Providing outdoor seating integrated with the site context.

Learning intent:
To connect taste, memory, and place in the visitor experience.

--------------------------------------------------
I) SERVICE AREA
--------------------------------------------------
Theme / focus:
Operations and site support.

Description:
The Service Area is dedicated to staffing, logistics, maintenance, and operational continuity. It supports public programming while minimizing disruption to sensitive heritage fabric.

Activities:
- Staff circulation and coordination.
- Maintenance and logistics support.

Learning intent:
To ensure long-term functionality and preservation standards.

==================================================================
4) CONTENT THEMES BY CATEGORY
==================================================================

Economic heritage:
- Pearling labor systems.
- Fishing and coastal livelihoods.
- Local trade and exchange networks.

Social heritage:
- Family routines and domestic organization.
- Majlis culture and hospitality.
- Intergenerational storytelling.

Environmental heritage:
- Material vulnerability of coral-stone settlements.
- Water and climate constraints.
- Adaptive land-use and cultivation methods.

Participatory heritage:
- Hands-on tools and craft engagement.
- Activity-based interpretation.
- Visitor contribution to memory archive.

==================================================================
5) IMPLEMENTATION CHARACTER
==================================================================

Conservation direction:
- Stabilize first, activate second.
- Use minimal, reversible interventions.
- Prioritize authenticity of materials and spatial character.

Visitor management:
- Clear route sequencing.
- Guided access and controlled movement.
- Capacity-sensitive operations to reduce wear on fragile areas.

Operational ecosystem:
- Collaboration with public institutions, educators, and cultural partners.
- Use of trained guides, conservators, and maintenance teams.
- Program sustainability through coordinated management and phased growth.

==================================================================
6) SHORT PROJECT SUMMARY
==================================================================

Tides of Time transforms Al Jumail from an abandoned coastal settlement into a living heritage system. Through preservation, careful adaptation, and participatory programming, the project allows visitors to understand pre-oil life in Qatar by experiencing its labor, family structures, social customs, and environmental realities directly across themed houses and support zones.
